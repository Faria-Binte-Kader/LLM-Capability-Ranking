Large Language Model Task Benchmark Ranking

Welcome to the LLM Capability Benchmark Ranking platform, designed to evaluate and rank Large Language Models (LLMs) based on their performance across a structured set of cognitive capabilities and fine-grained skills. Our evaluations cover 13 core capabilities — including Reasoning, Narrative Understanding, Ethical / Fair Response Generation, Adherence to Instructions, and more — spanning 82 individual benchmark datasets shared across all evaluated models. Each model's score is the average of its performance across all selected datasets, with higher scores indicating stronger overall capability.

Use the checkboxes on the left to select which capabilities and skills to include in the ranking. Expand any capability ► to see its constituent skills and toggle them on or off. On the right, click a model row to expand it and view a per-capability, per-skill, and per-dataset breakdown of its scores. For each skill, the score shown is the aggregate (sum) of its underlying dataset scores.

This ranking platform is developed by the Bridge-AI Lab, Department of Computer Science, University of Central Florida, as part of ongoing research into fairness, bias mitigation, and trustworthiness in large language models. All results are based on systematically designed benchmark datasets and transparent scoring criteria. Model scores represent averages across selected datasets at the specified temperature setting; models missing data for a given dataset are ranked based on the datasets for which results are available.
